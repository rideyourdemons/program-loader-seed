Analytics & Privacy

What analytics are for

Analytics help us understand which pages people visit, which tools get used, and whether content is clear. This is used to improve navigation and clarity. It is not used to pressure people or target vulnerabilities.

Google Analytics 4 (GA4)

This site may use Google Analytics 4 (GA4) to collect aggregated usage information such as page views and navigation paths. We treat analytics as observational—analytics do not control personal outcomes, claims, or persuasion.

What we track (high level)

  • Page views (which pages are visited).
  • Basic navigation (which pages are clicked next).
  • Aggregate engagement signals (e.g., general time-on-page).

What we do not track or do

  • We do not use analytics to infer your emotional state.
  • We do not use analytics to diagnose, label, or profile you psychologically.
  • We do not use analytics to trigger purchase prompts based on vulnerability.
  • We do not promise anonymity beyond what the underlying analytics platform provides.

Consent and regional expectations

Depending on your region, you may see consent choices for analytics. If consent is required by applicable rules in your region, analytics should not run until you choose.

Practical privacy note

No analytics system on the public internet should be treated as "perfect privacy." If you want maximum privacy, limit what you share online and avoid entering sensitive personal information into any website.
