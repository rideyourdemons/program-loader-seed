### Stress, Load, and the Body
- **Allostatic Load and Allostasis: Implications for Neuropsychopharmacology**  
  Overview of how chronic stress and ongoing demands create cumulative wear-and-tear on the body.  
  `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK350265/`

- **Protective and Damaging Effects of Stress Mediators**  
  Describes how long-term activation of the stress response system affects cardiovascular and metabolic health.  
  `https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/9800931/`

### Job Strain and Control
- **Job Strain as a Risk Factor for Coronary Heart Disease: A Collaborative Meta-Analysis**  
  Large-scale evidence linking high demands with low control at work to increased risk of heart disease.  
  `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3816699/`

### Stress, Emotion, and Decision-Making
- **Affect Labeling and Emotional Regulation**  
  Shows how putting feelings into words can reduce emotional intensity and support better regulation.  
  `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC3156609/`

- **Stress Response and Decision-Making**  
  Explains how stress shifts thinking toward fast, reactive systems and away from slower, reflective judgment.  
  `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/books/NBK541120/`

- **Stress Signalling Pathways that Impair Prefrontal Cortex Structure and Function**  
  Details how stress chemicals weaken brain regions involved in planning and impulse control.  
  `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2907136/`

- **Sleep and Emotion Regulation: Evidence from Functional Neuroimaging**  
  Discusses how sleep supports emotional regulation and more balanced decision-making.  
  `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2656292/`

### Masculinity, Help-Seeking, and Isolation
- **Men, Masculinity, and the Contexts of Help Seeking**  
  Explores how masculine norms influence when and how men reach out for support under strain.  
  `https://psycnet.apa.org/doi/10.1037/0003-066X.58.1.5`

- **Social Relationships and Health: A Flashpoint for Health Policy**  
  Summarizes evidence that social connection and perceived support reduce stress and improve health outcomes.  
  `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC6125010/`

- **Caregiver Burden and Mental Health Outcomes**  
  Examines how ongoing, often invisible responsibilities contribute to stress and health risks.  
  `https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC4056857/`
